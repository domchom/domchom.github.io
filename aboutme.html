Random Notes on Photos

​​​​​​​For digital images, anything before January 2025 was likely taken with the Ricoh GR III, and anything after with the Fujifilm X100VI. Most of my recent digital images are SOOC or have minimal edits since I usually prefer minimal effort when shooting digital.

For 35mm film, most of my shots are taken with a Canon A-1 and a Canon 50mm f/1.8 FD Mount lens. For 120 film, I have uses the Fujica GW690 - which has a fixed 90mm f/3.5 lens - and the Mamiya RB67 Pro-S. I meter my 120 film shots using Ansel Adams’ zone system and a Minolta Spotmeter F.

For film, I develop color negatives at home using the Cinestill C-41 kit and black-and-white film with Ilford Ilfosol 3.

I scan 35mm film either with a Plustek OpticFilm 7600i scanner or using a DIY copy-stand setup. For the copy stand, I use the Cinestill CS-Lite as a light source, a Canon EOS Rebel SL1, and a vintage Vivitar Macro 55mm f/2.8 1:1 lens.

120 film is scanned using the same copy-stand setup, but since the negatives are larger, I take multiple shots of different parts of the frame and stitch them together in Photoshop or Lightroom Classic. Negative scans are converted with the Negative Lab Pro Lightroom plugin, and I make minor adjustments to white balance, exposure, sharpening, contrat, dust removal, and any other small tweaks that feel necessary.​​​​​​​
